Why Jute

Climate & biology

  • Jute is a **fast-growing C3 plant**; cultivation sequesters CO₂ and releases O₂ during its short season.
  • Multiple sources (FAO, IJSG) note significant CO₂ absorption and O₂ release per hectare; exact figures vary by cultivar, yield and inputs.
  • Rain-fed in many regions → **low irrigation** vs cotton in arid zones.

Read more: FAO Future Fibres: Jute · International Jute Study Group (IJSG) archive

Socio-economic upside

  • Income for smallholders in developing countries; trade can act as **climate-positive demand** rather than distant compensation.
  • Durable jute totes create a **repairable asset**; worn units become **fibre feedstock** for new totes or board.
  • For Finland: import jute goods to **displace plastic** while exploring **domestic jute-processing** and fibre-based manufacturing jobs.

Strategic idea: channel part of climate-spend into **real demand for jute** (goods that displace plastics) rather than only offsets — building a scalable circular market.
